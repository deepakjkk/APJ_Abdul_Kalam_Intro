Scientific and Technological Contributions

From July 1992 to December 1999, A. P. J. Abdul Kalam served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of the Department of Defence Research & Development (DRDO). During this pivotal period, he played a significant political and technological role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests.

Innovation in Healthcare Medical Emergency guided by Dr ...A.P.J. Abdul Kalam significantly advanced healthcare innovation by developing the Kalam-Raju stent, a low-cost cardiac stent, and the Kalam-Raju tablet, a rugged device to aid rural healthcare workers. These innovations were driven by his vision of making advanced medical technology accessible to the common person, particularly in rural and underserved areas, to achieve an affordable and developed India..

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's "Technology Vision 2020" refers to the technological blueprint within the broader "India 2020" vision, aiming to transform India into a developed nation by the year 2020 through advancements in agriculture, education, health, technology, and infrastructure, focusing on self-reliance and integrated development to achieve economic and social prosperity.
